# A SHORT TECHNICAL HELICOPTER HISTORY

• Helicopter development came after fixed-wing aircraft
• Helicopters require more power than airplanes
• Helicopters withstand higher stresses
• Harder to control
• More moving parts
• Early helicopters lacked sufficient power
• Once powered → control problems appeared
• Once control understood → vibration and maintenance problems appeared
• Development progressed by overcoming obstacles one by one

• First practical helicopter: Focke-Wulf Fw-61 (1938)
• Weir W-5 flew in 1938
• Sikorsky VS-300 followed
• War accelerated helicopter development
• Anton Flettner and Heinrich Focke led German development
• Fa-223 crossed the English Channel (6 Sept 1945)

• After WWII → better understanding of dynamics and stability
• Helicopters became safer and less stressful to fly
• Bell 47 (1946) → first certified helicopter
• Sycamore (1952) → first certified British helicopter
• Light control forces, no power assistance

• Introduction of turbine engine → major breakthrough
• Turbine lighter than piston engine
• Fewer moving parts
• Greater payload
• Reduced maintenance

• First turbine helicopter: modified Kaman K-225 (1951)
• First twin turbine: Kaman (1954)
• Bell Huey flew in 1956

• 1960s → helicopter design mastered as a system
• Sikorsky S-61 and Boeing Chinook → long service life
• Few breakthroughs after 1960s
• Continuous refinement
• Composite materials reduced weight
• Extended service life
• Improved engines and transmissions

• Some failures catastrophic (gearboxes, rotor heads)
• Helicopters can survive engine failure
• Electronic monitoring improved safety
• Spontaneous failures rare

• HUMS = Health and Usage Monitoring System
• Measures stress and time applied
• Calculates remaining safe life of components

# APPLICATIONS OF THE HELICOPTER

• Can land almost anywhere
• No runway required
• Can hover
• Can land on water (amphibious types)
• Can transfer goods in hover
• Ideal rescue vehicle
• Used for war casualties
• Shipwreck rescue
• Mountain rescue
• Natural disaster response
• Can refuel from ships or oil rigs
• Operates in bad weather

• Originally designed for military use
• Vietnam War → major helicopter role
• Insert troops
• Withdraw troops
• Deliver ammunition and food
• Evacuate wounded
• Recover downed crews

# HELICOPTER MAIN COMPONENTS

• Airframe
• Fuselage
• Landing gear
• Power plant
• Transmission
• Main rotor system
• Tail rotor system

# AIRFRAME

• Fundamental structure
• Made of metal / wood / composite / combination
• Composite = fiber + resin layers
• Aluminum commonly used
• Stainless steel / titanium in high stress areas

# FUSELAGE

• Main body section
• Houses cabin
• Houses engine
• Houses transmission
• Houses avionics
• Houses flight controls
• Houses powerplant
• Transfers loads
• Protects occupants
• Provides visibility
• Fuel tanks near center of mass (CM)
• May float (amphibious)

• Early Bell 47 → steel tube lattice frame
• Stressed skin structure in tail cone
• Doors and windows prevent full load skin structure
• Pylon = welded tube frame
• Composite materials → lighter, well damped, long fatigue life

# LANDING GEAR

• Supports helicopter on ground
• Absorbs landing forces
• Fixed or retractable

Types:
• Wheels
• Skids
• Skis
• Floats

Retractable gear:
• Reduces drag
• Reduces fuel consumption
• Operated by hydraulic actuators

Wheeled gear includes:
• Tires
• Wheels
• Brakes
• Oleo struts
• Shock absorbers

Skid gear includes:
• Forward cross tube
• Aft cross tube
• Saddle assemblies
• Skid tubes
• Damper assemblies
• Abrasion strip

# POWER PLANT

Types:
• Piston engine
• Turbine engine

Turbine components:
• Compressor
• Combustion chamber
• Turbine
• Accessory gearbox

• 75% airflow used for cooling
• Exhaust does not produce forward thrust

# TRANSMISSION

• Transfers power from engine
• Drives main rotor
• Drives tail rotor
• Drives accessories

Components:
• Main rotor transmission
• Tail rotor drive system
• Clutch
• Freewheeling unit

• Reduces engine RPM to rotor RPM

# MAIN ROTOR SYSTEM

• Generates lift
• Consists of:
• Mast
• Hub
• Rotor blades

• Mast = hollow cylindrical shaft
• Hub = blade attachment point
• Rotor disc = circular swept area

# TAIL ROTOR SYSTEM

• Also called anti-torque rotor
• Counteracts main rotor torque
• Prevents fuselage spin
• Keeps helicopter straight

# IMPORTANT VOCABULARY

• withstand = resist
• overcome = defeat
• accelerate = speed up
• master = fully understand
• survive = continue operating after failure
• monitor = observe continuously
• certify = officially approve
• reduce = make smaller

• inherently = naturally
• pylon = structural support frame
• unduly = excessively
• lattice = crisscross framework
• trim = balance in flight
• vibration = rapid shaking
• canopy = cockpit cover
• damped = reduced vibration
• welded = joined by heat
• framework = supporting structure

# FAILURE ANALYSIS TERMS

• fail = stop functioning
• rear cross tube = back transverse tube
• flange = projecting rim
• spring tube = elastic tube element
• clamp = holding device
• stress corrosion cracking = cracking due to stress + corrosion
